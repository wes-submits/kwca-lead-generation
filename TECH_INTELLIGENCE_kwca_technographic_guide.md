## Keyword Custom Audiences — Tech Stack Targeting

## Executive Summary

This guide provides technographic targeting criteria for identifying high-fit accounts based on their marketing technology stack. Use these signals to prioritize accounts, personalize outreach, and identify integration opportunities.

**What is Technographic Intelligence?**
- Analysis of a company's marketing/advertising technology stack
- Signals of data maturity and buying readiness
- Integration pathway mapping
- Competitive displacement opportunities

**How to Use This Guide**
1. **Account Prioritization**: Score accounts by tech stack fit
2. **Outreach Personalization**: Reference specific technologies
3. **Integration Planning**: Map kwca to their existing stack
4. **Competitive Positioning**: Identify displacement opportunities

## COMPETITIVE DISPLACEMENT OPPORTUNITIES

### Scenario 1: Legacy DMP (Adobe AAM, Lotame)

**Signal**: Company using legacy DMP + announcing "data strategy refresh"

**Approach**:
> "Many companies are sunsetting their DMPs in favor of more flexible data infrastructure. Is {{company}} evaluating alternatives? We can provide the audience data layer without the overhead of a full DMP."

### Scenario 2: Demographic-Only Data Provider

**Signal**: Using Experian, Acxiom demographic data only

**Approach**:
> "Demographic targeting works, but intent-based audiences typically outperform by 30-50%. Are you layering any intent signals on top of your demographic data?"

### Scenario 3: Single-Channel Data

**Signal**: Strong Facebook presence, no programmatic

**Approach**:
> "{{company}} clearly knows how to scale on Facebook. Are you looking to diversify into programmatic or CTV? We can provide the same high-intent audiences for those channels."

### Scenario 4: Platform Native Audiences Only

**Signal**: No third-party data visible, using only Meta/LinkedIn native targeting

**Approach**:
> "Platform native audiences are convenient but limited. Most sophisticated marketers layer in third-party intent data for better precision. Is that something {{company}} is exploring?"
